Madhya Pradesh is a central state of India, often called the “Heart of India” because of its geographical location. Its capital city is Bhopal and its largest city is Indore. The state was formed on 1 November 1956 and Hindi is the main language spoken there. Madhya Pradesh is famous for its rich history, culture, and wildlife. Important tourist attractions include the Khajuraho Group of Monuments, known for their beautiful temple carvings, the Sanchi Stupa, an important Buddhist site, and national parks like Kanha National Park and Bandhavgarh National Park, which are famous for tigers. The state has important rivers such as the Narmada, Tapti, and Chambal, and its economy mainly depends on agriculture, especially crops like wheat and soybean.
